Special Projects

Our 2026–2027 annual plan brings together workshops, summits, community fairs, research, and media collaborations—all designed to promote psychological literacy and youth mental well-being across Hong Kong.

2026–2027 Programme

Confirmed dates

HKSPI Mental Health Booth

In collaboration with Companion HK · HKSPI Psychology & Mental Health Fair 2026

An annual event dedicated to promoting psychological literacy and raising awareness about mental health from a youth-centric perspective. Theme: Mental Health Village.

  • Date: 1–2 August 2026
  • Time: 10:00–22:00
  • Venue: Kai Tak Airside (3/F)
  • Expected reach: ~30,000 visitors (children, youth, families, and mental health professionals)

HKSPI (Hong Kong Society of Psychological Innovation) is Hong Kong's largest psychology-focused NGO under the Societies Ordinance, run by psychology students and graduates from institutions including Ivy League and Oxbridge universities. The 2025 fair welcomed 5,000+ visitors and 50+ collaborating partners.

Music & Mental Health Workshop

In collaboration with In Harmony HK

Theme: Find Your Harmony: Voice, Mind & Well-being

Empowering secondary school students to use music and singing for emotional expression, stress relief, and resilience. Planned for summer holidays; programme details to be finalised with In Harmony HK.

  • Sound & Color Resonance Journey — linking singing bowls, colour, and emotions for stress relief
  • Musical Imposter Charades — movement-based group activity building observation and bonding
  • Estimated participants: ~50 students
Dates to be confirmed

Mental Health Workshop — Neurodiversity

In collaboration with Neurodiversity HK

Theme: Different Minds, Same Strength: Embracing Neurodiversity & Mental Well-being

Raising awareness and acceptance of neurodiversity (ADHD, autism, dyslexia, and more) among secondary school students. Planned for summer holidays.

  • Sensory Strengths Exploration — stations exploring light, sound, texture, and puzzles
  • Neurodivergence Quiz Challenge — team-based learning to build awareness and correct misconceptions
  • Estimated participants: ~50 students

2026 Mental Health Youth Summit

Theme: Self and Social Stigmatisation

Panel discussions and a Human Library session exploring how internal and external stigma affect young people's mental health, with practical guidance on reducing their impact.

  • Date: 10 July 2026 · 15:00–16:40
  • Venue: Chapel & President Chi-tung Yung Memorial Building, CUHK
  • Audience: ~100 secondary school and undergraduate students
  • Confirmed panelists include Dr. Lok Chi Wing, Dr. Stephanie Ng, Mr Jonathan Mok, Mr Michael Chan, and Miss Sophie Lee

Interview Series

Six episodes and growing—featuring DSE top scorers, musicians, individuals with lived mental health experience, and people with special educational needs (SEN). Each conversation explores achievement and adversity, pressure behind success, and realities often unseen by the public.

Upcoming episodes will feature athletes and mental health advocates. Be Positive HK amplifies authentic voices to normalise mental health conversations for young people navigating expectations and identity.
